THE CORE MISSION 
Participants must design and build a working prototype of an AI-powered, retention-first learning system that 
acts as a proactive cognitive companion for students preparing for JEE, NEET, or UPSC. 
The system must go beyond content delivery. It must remember what a student studied, adapt to their pace, 
and act autonomously to ensure nothing is forgotten. 
SECTION 1  ·  THE CHALLENGE 
What Participants Must Build 
Teams must build a working prototype addressing at least 3 of the 5 core modules below. Teams addressing all 5 score 
higher. 
1.  Spaced Repetition & Retention Engine 
A system tracking every concept a student studies and using a forgetting-curve algorithm to automatically schedule the 
next revision. Must maintain a dynamic learner knowledge graph. 
2.  Autonomous Study Planner 
An AI agent that generates a personalized daily study plan based on exam date, syllabus coverage, weak areas, and past 
performance — auto-rebalancing without any manual input. 
3.  Adaptive Practice & Question Generation 
Generates unlimited topic-wise and concept-wise practice questions using PYQ patterns. Detects weak areas through 
time-on-question and error analysis, then generates targeted similar questions. 
4.  AI Tutor with RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) 
A doubt-solving assistant answering questions in real time using trusted educational sources (NCERT, standard books). 
Responses must be grounded — not hallucinated. Voice support in Hindi/Hinglish is a bonus. 
5.  Multimodal Content Processor 
Takes a YouTube educational video URL, removes distractions, generates structured visual notes or a concept summary, 
and optionally dubs or subtitles it in an Indic language.

 SECTION 4  ·  THE DIFFERENTIATING FACTOR 
The Final Twist — Top 3 Showdown 
 
⚡  WHAT MAKES THIS HACKATHON DIFFERENT 
After the main judging round, the Top 3 teams do not simply celebrate. They enter a final "Cross-Audit" 
round where each team gets full access to their rivals' submitted projects — and the leaderboard is not 
final until this round is over. 
 
How the Cross-Audit Round Works 
 
1.  Project Swap 
The 3 finalists are assigned each other's fully submitted GitHub repositories, demo builds, and technical reports. Each 
team gets 45 minutes to explore and audit the other two projects in a closed room — no interaction with the rival team 
allowed. 
2.  Finding the Gap 
Each team must identify at least one concrete missing implementation in each rival's project — a feature, module, or AI 
capability that is absent or incomplete in that project but is fully built and functional in their own. 
3.  The Claim Presentation 
Each team gets 5 minutes to present their findings to the judges. They must: (a) clearly demonstrate the gap in the 
rival's project, and (b) live-demo that their own project has a working implementation of that exact feature. 
4.  Leaderboard Shuffle 
Judges validate each claim. For every successfully proven and accepted claim, the claiming team earns +10 Shuffle 
Points. Final positions are determined by:  
Final Score  =  Main Judging Score  +  Shuffle Points earned  −  Shuffle Points lost 
(Shuffle Points are lost if a claim made against your project is validated by judges)

Rules & Boundaries 
▸  Claims must be about implemented functionality — not UI polish, color choices, or documentation gaps. 
▸  A claim is valid only if the claimant can live-demo the working feature in their own project during the 5-minute slot. 
▸  Each team can make a maximum of 2 claims per rival project (4 total claims across both rivals). 
▸  Judges' validation decision is final. Frivolous or incorrect claims result in −5 points to the claimant. 
▸  Ties after shuffle points are broken by the team with the higher Technical Implementation score. 
 
Why This Twist Exists 
Building a good prototype is not enough — great engineers also know how to read code they didn't write, identify 
weaknesses under pressure, and defend their own design decisions when challenged. The Cross-Audit round tests 
exactly these skills, making the final result a true measure of both what was built and how deeply it was understood. 
It also means there is no "safe" second place — any of the top 3 teams can take first right up until the last minute.
